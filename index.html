❖ common name - Silver oak tree

❖ Scientific name - Grevillea robusta

❖ Classification -

Kingdom - Plantae

Family - Proteaceae

Order - Proteales

Mode of propagation:

Silver Oak can be propagated through seeds, cuttings, or tissue culture. Seed propagation is the most common method. Seeds are collected from mature trees and sown in nurseries. The seedlings are then transplanted into the field once they reach a suitable size. This method ensures genetic diversity in the plantation.

Uses:

Silver Oak is primarily cultivated for its high-quality timber. The wood is renowned for its durability, resistance to decay, and attractive grain patterns. It is used in various applications, including furniture making, construction, and veneer production. The timber is a valuable resource and is considered sustainable when harvested responsibly. The cultivation of Silver Oak offers several environmental benefits. The trees play a crucial role in carbon sequestration and help mitigate the effects of climate change. They also provide habitat and food for various wildlife species, contributing to biodiversity conservation.

  • Soil Type: Thrives in well-drained, sandy, or loamy soils with slightly acidic to neutral pH levels. It does not do well in waterlogged or saline soils.

Life Span:

  • Life Span: Typically lives around 50 to 65 years, though it may live longer in ideal conditions. It grows quickly, reaching maturity in a relatively short time.

Some Diseases that affect trees growth:

  • Pests: Susceptible to aphids, scale insects, and borers, especially when young.
  • Diseases: Root rot can be an issue in poorly drained soils. Fungal diseases like leaf spots may also affect it.

Medical Uses:

  • Anti-inflammatory: Leaves contain compounds that exhibit mild anti-inflammatory properties.
  • Antimicrobial: Various plant parts are used traditionally for their antimicrobial properties.
  • Wound Healing: Leaves and bark have been used in poultices to aid wound healing in some indigenous practices.
  • Skin Care: Used in some areas as a topical treatment for skin rashes and insect bites.
